Case: Rohan fell for a captive-portal phishing / evil twin Wi-Fi trick. He entered his email and password on a fake login page while on public Wi-Fi, letting an attacker harvest his credentials. On insecure public Wi-Fi, attackers can also attempt man-in-the-middle (MITM) snooping.

What went wrong:

  • Trusted a public network without verifying it.
  • Entered credentials on an unverified login page.
  • Used public Wi-Fi without extra protection (e.g., VPN/2FA).

Threat(s) involved:

  • Phishing via fake Wi-Fi portal (credential harvesting).
  • MITM risk on open networks.

How to stay safe (Lesson of the Week):

  • Verify the network name with staff; beware look-alike SSIDs.
  • Avoid logging in to important accounts on public Wi-Fi; prefer mobile data/hotspot.
  • If you must use public Wi-Fi: use a VPN, and ensure sites show HTTPS (lock icon).
  • Enable 2FA on email, banking, and social apps to limit damage.
  • Never enter passwords on pop-ups or portals you didn‚Äôt expect.

If this happens to you (Do this now):

  • Change the password of any account you entered there (and others using the same/similar password).
  • Enable 2FA immediately.
  • Check recent activity and sign out of active sessions.
  • Delete/forget the rogue network from your device.
  • Consider using a VPN for future public Wi-Fi use.
