Firewall Friday — Answer Key 📝

🔐 Decode the Emoji – Answers

  • 1️⃣ 📡👂 → Eavesdropping / Wiretapping
    👉 Secretly listening to communications (on networks or calls) to capture sensitive information.
  • 2️⃣ 🛑🧑‍💻🚫 → Firewall
    👉 A network barrier that blocks or filters unauthorized traffic to protect systems.
  • 3️⃣ 📱🔓👀 → Shoulder Surfing
    👉 Physically watching someone type passwords/PINs (often on phones or ATMs) to steal credentials.
  • 4️⃣ 💰🛑🔗 → Ransomware
    👉 Malware that encrypts or locks files and demands payment to restore access.
  • 5️⃣ 📤🐛 → Worm
    👉 A self-replicating piece of malware that spreads across networks without user action.
  • 6️⃣ 📧📢🚮 → Spam
    👉 Unwanted/junk email — often used as a delivery method for scams and phishing.
  • 7️⃣ 🕵️‍♂️🌐🔍 → OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence)
    👉 Collecting useful information from publicly available sources (social media, websites, records) for analysis.

🎣✅ Phish or Legit? – Answers

  • 1️⃣ “Dear User, your account will be closed in 24 hours. Click this link to verify your details immediately.”
    👉 Phish 🎣 – Uses urgency and vague greeting (“Dear User”) to trick people into clicking malicious links.
  • 2️⃣ “Amazon: Your order #548321 has been shipped. Track it here in your Amazon app.”
    👉 Legit ✅ – Real order notifications usually come through the official app or website. (Safe only if from Amazon directly.)
  • 3️⃣ “Congratulations! You’ve been selected for a government grant. Provide your bank details to claim.”
    👉 Phish 🎣 – Government bodies never ask for bank details over email/SMS to give grants. Classic scam.
  • 4️⃣ “Your friend sent you a Google Drive link to photos from yesterday’s event.”
    👉 Legit ✅ (if confirmed) – Likely safe if expected and from a known contact. Always verify before clicking.
  • 5️⃣ “PayPal: We noticed unusual activity. Please confirm your identity by logging in at pay-pal.verify.com.”
    👉 Phish 🎣 – The fake domain “pay-pal.verify.com” is a giveaway. Real PayPal uses only paypal.com.
